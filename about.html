Who We Are

Polaris Academic Agents is a study abroad guidance agency based in Kisumu, Kenya. We are licensed by the Commission for University Education (CUE) as a Student Recruitment Agency, and we operate under the ICEF Code of Conduct and the London Statement on the Ethical Recruitment of International Students.[cite: 1]

We share what the brochures don't tell you. We ask the questions other agents skip. And when we give you advice, it is backed by deep knowledge, genuine care, and the hard lessons of lived experience.[cite: 2]

Our role is to help you get there. Not to hijack the journey.[cite: 2]

Purpose

To ensure that no student steps into an international education journey unprepared, misled, or alone.[cite: 2]

Vision

A world where every ambitious student has access to world-class, honest guidance that matches their goals, their context, and their values.[cite: 2]

Mission

To provide full-journey study abroad guidance - from first conversation to first week abroad - rooted in lived experience, radical honesty, and a genuine desire to see every student succeed on their own terms.[cite: 2]

Brand Pillars & Messaging

Honest Guidance

We tell you what others won't. Our advice is never shaped by which schools pay us the most commission. Every recommendation is student-first, backed by knowledge and experience. Polaris earns from your trust, not your confusion.[cite: 2]

Full-Journey Support

We don't disappear after the visa is stamped. We prepare you for what life actually looks and feels like from packing the right clothes to setting up your first week for success. The destination is not the finish line. Arrival is the starting line.[cite: 2]

Lived Experience

Our guidance comes from someone who has made this journey - including the hard parts. That is the most valuable thing we offer. We were one of them. Now we make sure the next one is better prepared.[cite: 2]

Your Journey, Your Way

We help you get there. We don't choose for you. The journey only has value when you make it of your own volition and complete it with your own effort. Our role is to clear the path, not to walk it for you.[cite: 2]
